# $5K in 30 Days — Strategy & Execution Plan

**Operator profile:** 10+ yrs Product Management, enterprise SaaS, sales/BD, AI automation (ChatGPT, Claude, Make.com, Apify, Apollo), healthcare + recruiting/staffing industry experience.

**Hard constraints:** $50 starting budget. The business must pay for itself — revenue funds tooling, never the reverse. 10–20 hrs/week. No SaaS development. High-ticket services. Launchable this weekend. Scalable.

**Implication of the $50 budget:** the cold email infrastructure in this repo (~$360 month 1: domains + Zapmail + Prospeo + Smartlead) is **not** a startup cost. First revenue comes from $0 channels — LinkedIn, warm network, and low-volume manual email from your existing inbox. Client deposit #1 buys the infrastructure. The repo is your *fulfillment system*, purchased with client money.

## Elimination

**Cut Idea 3 (Fractional PM).** It's the most comfortable idea — which is exactly the warning sign. It best matches the résumé and worst matches the 30-day clock. Fractional leadership is bought on trust; cold outreach for it converts terribly; and unless a warm contact is already in-market, the first close lands after day 30. It also fails the scalability constraint: it is hours-for-dollars with a ceiling of one person's calendar.

**Cut Idea 2 (AI Sprints).** Challenged assumption: "everyone wants AI automation" ≠ "anyone is searching for a $1,750 automation sprint." The pain is real but unfelt as a line item — you must *educate* before you sell, and education burns days you don't have. Three sales of a fuzzy offer beats zero sales of nothing, but it's strictly harder than two sales of Idea 1, where the deliverable ("client meetings on your calendar") needs no explanation. Non-recurring revenue also fails the scale test.

## The Winner: Done-For-You AI Outbound for Recruiting & Staffing Agencies

**Why this one wins:**

1. **The buyer already wants the exact output.** Agency owners don't need convincing that client meetings are valuable — one new client is worth $20–80K in placement fees. You're selling money at a discount, not a concept.
2. **Unfair advantage stack.** Recruiting industry fluency (you speak their language and know their pain cold) + sales/BD chops (you run the calls) + AI automation skills + **this repo, which is literally a complete 28-skill fulfillment system** for the service being sold (list building, copy, deliverability, sending, scoring). Almost no competitor selling lead-gen to staffing agencies has all four.
3. **Math is the easiest:** 2 closes = $5K. Every other idea needs more sales or slower sales.
4. **Self-funding by design.** Sales happen on free channels. Client #1's ~$1,250 deposit funds the ~$360 delivery stack with margin to spare. $50 budget is never threatened.
5. **Scales past 30 days.** Recurring retainers, productized delivery, and the fulfillment is mostly automated — capacity grows by adding campaigns, not hours. At 5 clients this is a $7.5K+/mo agency.

## 30-Day Execution Plan

**Revenue target:** 2 clients × $2,500 month-1 invoice (=$1,000 setup + $1,500 retainer) = **$5,000**. Cash collected if only 50% deposits land in-month: $2,500 — so the *operating target is 3 signed clients* to make $5K cash collection probable. Plan the activity for 3, celebrate at 2.

### Phase 0 — Launch Weekend (Days 1–2) · Spend: $0

- **Day 1 (Sat):** Write the one-page offer (outcome, price, 2-founding-client framing). Rewrite LinkedIn headline/about to the niche promise. Set up Calendly free tier. Draft the 4-touch outreach sequence (connect note → email → follow-up → breakup).
- **Day 2 (Sun):** Build prospect list #1: 100 staffing/recruiting agency owner names via LinkedIn search + Apollo free tier. Message 15 warm contacts from your recruiting past. Send first 20 LinkedIn connection requests + 10 manual emails. Write LinkedIn post #1 ("After 10 years in recruiting/SaaS, here's why most staffing agencies' BD is broken — and what AI changes").

### Phase 1 — Sell (Days 3–14) · Spend: ≤$50 (optional: Calendly/Loom upgrades only if needed)

Daily routine (~2 hrs/day):
- 25 LinkedIn connection requests with note (250–300 over the phase)
- 12 manual 1:1 emails from your own inbox (no infra needed at this volume)
- Reply to every response within 2 hours; push to a 15-min call
- LinkedIn post Mon/Wed/Fri; comment on 5 staffing-industry posts daily

Milestones: **first discovery call by Day 5–7, first close by Day 10–14.** On signing: collect 50% deposit (~$1,250) immediately.

### Phase 2 — Build with Client Money (Days 11–18) · Spend: ~$360, funded by deposit #1

Stand up the minimum stack from this repo: domains via `/zapmail-domain-setup-public` (~$240), Smartlead starter ($39/mo), Zapmail inboxes (~$60/mo), Prospeo export (~$20). Start warmup immediately — it needs 14–21 days, which is why this begins the day the deposit clears, not later. Meanwhile:
- Run `/icp-onboarding` + `/cold-email-kickoff` for client #1 → `campaign-plan.md`
- Build their list (`/prospeo-full-export`, grade with `/list-quality-scorecard`)
- Write copy (`/campaign-copywriting`, `/spam-word-checker`)
- **Bridge delivery:** launch LinkedIn outbound from the client's profile (zero warmup needed) so they see activity and meetings in week 1, not week 4
- Keep Phase 1 selling routine running at ~60% volume — clients #2 and #3 come from this

### Phase 3 — Deliver + Close the Gap (Days 19–30)

- Upload and launch client #1's campaign as DRAFT via `/smartlead-campaign-upload-public`; start sending as warmup allows
- Weekly client check-in with a simple metrics snapshot (use `/positive-reply-scoring` logic)
- Close clients #2–3 from the pipeline built in Phases 1–2; each new signing = +$1,250 cash immediately
- Day 30: 2–3 signed clients, $2,500–$5,000+ collected, $5,000–$7,500 booked, delivery system running, case study #1 in progress

### Daily Activity Math (working backwards from $5K)

Conversion assumptions (conservative, blended LinkedIn + manual email to a warm-ish niche you have credibility in):

| Stage | Rate | Needed |
|---|---|---|
| Signed clients | — | 3 |
| Proposals/offers made | 50% close (founder-led, high-intent niche) | 6 |
| Discovery calls held | 60% call → proposal | 10 |
| Positive replies | 50% positive reply → call held (incl. no-shows) | 20 |
| Total outreach touches | ~4% touch → positive reply | **~500** |

**500 touches ÷ 20 working days = 25 touches/day** (e.g., 15 LinkedIn connects/messages + 10 manual emails), plus follow-ups (~10/day). Time cost: ~1.5–2 hrs/day on outreach + ~3–4 hrs/week on calls + (from Day 11) ~4–5 hrs/week on fulfillment = **12–18 hrs/week. Inside budget.**

If by Day 10 the positive-reply rate is below ~2%, the offer framing is the problem, not the volume — rewrite the hook (lead with the case-study discount or a specific meeting guarantee) before scaling activity.

### Non-negotiables

- **Deposit before work.** 50% on signature, no exceptions. This is what keeps the business self-funding.
- **Don't build before the first sale.** No domains, no inboxes, no tooling until deposit #1 clears.
- **One niche.** Staffing/recruiting agencies only for 30 days. Healthcare staffing counts; "any B2B company" does not.
- **Sell the bridge honestly.** Month 1 = build + launch + LinkedIn-sourced meetings; email-sourced meetings ramp in weeks 3–6. Set this expectation in the proposal so the retainer renews.
